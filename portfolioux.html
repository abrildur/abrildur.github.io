Bumble (Usability Testing)

Brief summary: Bumble

This report presents the results and findings obtained from the application of usability tests on the Bumble platform, with the objective of investigating the way in which users interact with the application, based on their motivations and contexts of use.

The test was conducted by six researchers and testers from Sperientia's LabW [studio+lab]®. Seven participants with free and premium accounts were recruited by Sperientia [studio+lab]® in order to investigate their experience and perception of the use of this application. For the usability test, three tasks were determined for the users within the dating application.

From the execution of this study, it was found that users prioritize photos in the users with whom they choose to initiate an interaction, finally, findings were identified that cross the level of usability and disrupt the use of digital products in a given context, highlighting that within the intrinsic and extrinsic motivations were discovered classist practices around the standard of body image and status according to the location of users in certain areas.

Role: UX Researcher (recruitment, observation, research design and cover design)

Challenges:
-> Generate empathy during usability test facilitation.
-> Generate usability test patterns collaboratively.
-> Recruitment of people for Usability testing.

Brief summary: Exíto

This evaluation aims to recognize and show the usability problems of the website of one of the largest online stores in Colombia: Éxito.com

This study involved the participation of six evaluators from Sperientia's Lab W: [Studio+Lab]®, whose purpose was to identify possible problems that may arise for users of the analyzed platforms.

The usability problems could be recognized in the following screens: home page, product search and selection, and payment, which show that the interface lacks some basics of user-centered design. This may affect the way in which users perform their tasks and the fulfillment of their objectives within the online store.

These problems could cause the image of the company or its website to be affected by the user's experience during their stay on the website.

Role: UX Researcher (Expert Evaluator, research design and cover design).

15 Usability Problems (Expert Evaluation)

Brief summary: 15 usability problems

The objective of this heuristic research is to identify possible usability problems that may occur for users of the analyzed platforms 15 Peruvian, Mexican, Colombian, and American platforms. The report includes problems detected in digital platforms of companies and organizations such as Bancolombia, Banco Falabella Colombia, Banorte Móvil (Mexico), Ixhua (Mexico), Clínica Cayetano Heredia (Peru), Houndbox (Colombia), Skechers (Peru), Aitraveltours (Peru), Cyberia Networks (Peru) and other cases.

This study involved the participation of six evaluators from Sperientia's Lab W: [Studio+Lab]®, whose purpose was to identify possible problems that may arise for users of the analyzed platforms. The usability problems of the different websites in Peru, Mexico, and Colombia were evaluated. Prioritizing those that could be more relevant to the business.

Among the most important findings, we highlight that a great variety of systems have in many cases more than one usability problem. This indicates that many of the fundamentals of user-centered design are overlooked, which can significantly affect the tasks that users need to perform within these interfaces. Not all of these websites or applications take into consideration aspects of accessibility and inclusion. Finally, the image of these companies or sites can be affected by the user's experience with these platforms, which is not always optimal.

Role: UX Researcher (Expert Evaluator, research design and cover design).

-> Collaboration and adaptation for reporting results.
-> Finding theoretical references to support the findings of the inspection.

Project related to Citizen Experience (Expert Evaluation)

Report and references

Bibliographical references: Sperientia[Studio+Lab] LabW (2021), Expert Evaluation: Citizen Experience related project, Sperientia[studio+lab], Mexico.

Brief summary: Project related to Citizen Experience.

The case to inspect with heuristic evaluation methodology was a website related to Citizen Experience. The objective of the research was to identify compliance with the Citizen Experience-related guidelines applied on the website.

This heuristic evaluation involved the participation of six evaluators from Sperientia's Lab W: [Studio+Lab]®, whose purpose was to identify possible problems that may arise for users of the analyzed platforms.

One of the principal findings encountered are the following: the search results are classified in terms that do not facilitate understanding and there are variations in the information presented in the documents on the website, these problems could cause the citizen to have difficulties at the time of accessing the required information so that subsequently the relationship with the Citizen would be affected.

Role: UX Researcher (Expert Evaluator, research design and cover design).

Falabella vs Ripley: Summative study with unmoderated usability tests.

Brief summary

This case study is about a quantitative summative study conducted in March 2021, where unmoderated usability tests were performed on the websites of the Peruvian e-commerce stores Falabella and Ripley in their desktop version.

This study was conducted to compare the usability and user experience of these platforms based on a series of specific tasks and objectives, covering both their main online shopping service, as well as other services that both platforms offer within the diversification of their business lines.

To achieve the purpose of this study, participants were asked to perform four tasks on the two websites.This study involved the participation of six evaluators from Sperientia's Lab W: [Studio+Lab]®.

Finally, it is concluded that Ripley's website offers a better user experience compared to Falabella's website according to the objectives and tasks set for this summative study.

Role: UX Researcher (Recluting, Evaluator, research design, cover design).
